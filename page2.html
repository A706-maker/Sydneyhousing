The First-Home Buyer Crisis

Income vs. Housing Cost

In March 2002, the cost of an average house in Sydney was 8.3 times that of the average annual full-time wage. While this is of course expensive, the rate at which it has risen makes it look incredibly cheap in comparison. In June 2024, the price of an average dwelling in Sydney is now equivalent to 14.4 years of earning the average wage in NSW. To put it simply, residents in Sydney need to pay $615,000 more in today's dollars than they would have 20 years ago.

Mortgage Stress and Affordability

As housing prices increase across Sydney, affordability rates decrease, therefore increasing mortgage stress amongst buyers. As of early 2026, mortgage stress remains high, with approximately 1.18 million to 1.6 million mortgage holders (24%–30%) considered to be at risk of mortgage stress, defined as paying more than 30% of their disposable income on housing. While stress levels may have decreased in 2026 from the peak levels reached in 2024–2025, they remain a top financial worry, heavily impacting lower-income households. Sydney is one of the most affected cities, with entry-level housing requiring over 57% of income to service.
