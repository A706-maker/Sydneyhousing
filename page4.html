What Can Actually Fix This?

From federal policy reforms to affordable suburb alternatives — here are the most discussed interventions to address Sydney's housing affordability crisis.

"Help to Buy" Scheme

The "Help to Buy" scheme assists eligible middle to low earners to purchase a home through a contribution from the Australian Government of up to 40% of the purchase price for new homes and 30% for existing homes. Eligible participants will be able to purchase property for up to $1.3 million in Sydney and regional centres, and up to $800,000 in the rest of NSW, with the requirement that the home be their principal place of residence. With about one-third available to buyers in NSW, approximately 10,000 places will be allocated across the country.

Negative Gearing

Negative gearing occurs when the costs of owning an investment (such as interest, maintenance, and rates) exceed the income it generates. This creates a net loss, which investors are able to deduct from their other income (e.g. salary) so that they pay less tax, acting as a long-term strategy for potential capital growth. This method is not risk-free, as the investor must have enough cash to cover the shortfall — making it risky if interest rates suddenly rise or the property is vacant. As tax laws are complex and subject to change, independent financial advice is recommended.

Social Housing

Social housing provides secure and affordable housing for people with very-low to low incomes. It includes public properties managed or owned by Homes NSW, while community housing properties are managed or owned by not-for-profit Community Housing Providers (e.g. Evolve Housing).

To be eligible for social housing you must meet the following criteria:

  • Be 18 or older
  • Provide proof of identity
  • Be an Australian citizen or permanent resident
  • Repay any previous social housing debts
  • Not own property or assets that meet your housing needs
  • Have a household income within the eligibility limit
